Rehabilitation for Elderly Frailty

Patients struggling with bed mobility, such as turning or repositioning themselves, often need moderate to significant assistance. Physiotherapy is instrumental in enhancing bed mobility through customised rehabilitation programs. These programs focus on teaching patients techniques to move from lying to sitting positions either with help or independently, thus improving their self-reliance in performing basic daily activities.

For those facing challenges in transitioning from sitting to standing or in bearing weight, physiotherapy introduces specific exercises and strength-building routines. The goal of these exercises is to develop the strength and stability necessary for patients to confidently support their own weight and make transfers with little to no assistance. Physiotherapists play a key role in motivating and aiding patients to enhance their walking abilities, encouraging them towards achieving independence in mobility.

Our thorough rehabilitation programs are also tailored for patients who are particularly frail, offering additional support for musculoskeletal concerns. Our services are designed to cater to the diverse needs of each patient, whether it involves improving bed mobility, walking abilities, or overcoming other mobility challenges. We aim to optimize functionality and enrich the quality of life for our patients through physiotherapy.

Furthermore, we conduct assessments to identify the most appropriate equipment for transfers, significantly reducing the risk of injuries to both patients and caregivers. These assessments lead to recommendations on transfer aids and equipment, customized for each patient's requirements, ensuring transfers are conducted safely and efficiently. Committed to exceptional patient care, we provide a complimentary assessment plan for all patients, enabling them to receive personalized recommendations at no extra cost.
